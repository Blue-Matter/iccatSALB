6  Performance Metrics

Resolution 24-09 (ICCAT 2024) describes the initial operational management objectives for Southern Atlantic Albacore, organised into four categories: Stock Status, Safety, Yield, and Stability.

Each performance metric (PM) below is calculated over the full 30-year projection period by default, for a given CMP, and can be summarised at two levels: for an individual OM, or across a set of OMs (e.g. the Reference Set).

In the latter case, the simulations from every OM in the set are first combined into a single pooled MSE object and the summary statistic (e.g. a probability or a mean) is then calculated once over that combined set of simulations. This allows CMP performance to be summarised both by individual uncertainty axis (e.g. by growth or natural mortality level) and overall, across the full Reference OM set.

Stock Status. The stock should have a 60% or greater probability of occurring in the green quadrant of the Kobe matrix over a 30-year projection period: i.e. \(SB > SB_{MSY}\) and \(F < F_{MSY}\) simultaneously.

Safety. There should be no greater than 15% probability of the stock falling below \(B_{LIM}\) at any point during the 30-year projection period, using an interim \(B_{LIM}\) of 40% of \(SB_{MSY}\) (footnote 1 of the Resolution). So that higher values are better for every PM, this is expressed as the probability of the stock remaining at or above \(B_{LIM}\) throughout the projection period, with a target of at least 85%.

Yield. The objective is to maximise overall catch levels. As the Resolution does not specify a particular statistic, the mean annual catch is used.

Stability. Any change in total allowable catch (TAC) between management periods should be 20% or less, noting that asymmetric stability limits (e.g. different thresholds for TAC increases versus decreases) may also be evaluated. This is expressed as the probability that the change in TAC between successive management cycles is less than 20%; i.e. calculated between the TAC values actually set every 3 years.
